## Daily Routine

### Morning Scan (15-20 min)

1. Check source list for new absurd stories
2. Log promising stories in story bank
3. Note initial joke ideas

### Story Criteria

Ask yourself:

- [ ] Is this ACTUALLY TRUE? (Verify!)
- [ ] Is it inherently absurd?
- [ ] Can I explain it in 2 sentences?
- [ ] Will it still be relevant by air date?
- [ ] Can I get at least 2 jokes from it?

## Types of Absurd News

### The "Why Would Anyone" Story
Someone did something inexplicably weird.
*"Man calls 911 because his cat won't let him in the house"*

### The Bureaucratic Absurdity
Rules/systems create ridiculous outcomes.
*"City fines homeowner for grass being 1/4 inch too long"*

### The Backfire
Plan goes comically wrong.
*"Bank robber's getaway car gets booted while inside"*

### The Coincidence
Reality being stranger than fiction.
*"Man finds his own missing car while stealing another car"*

### The Science WTF
Research with absurd implications.
*"Study finds pigeons can distinguish Monet from Picasso"*

### The Local News Gem
Small-town news being peak comedy.
*"Goat elected mayor for third consecutive term"*

### The Correction/Update
Story gets more absurd as details emerge.
*"UPDATE: The bear was also drunk"*

## Verification

**ALWAYS verify stories before using.**

- Check multiple sources
- Look for original reporting
- Watch for satire sites (The Onion, etc.)
- If it seems TOO perfect, dig deeper

**Credibility > Comedy**

A fake story will destroy trust. Real absurd news is abundant enough.

# Three-Act Outline

## Act One: Setup (Pages 1-30)

### Opening Image (p. 1-3)
*Visual that establishes the world and tone*

### Setup / Ordinary World (p. 1-10)
*Introduce protagonist, their flaw, their world*

### Theme Stated (p. 5-10)
*Someone says the theme (protagonist ignores it)*

### Catalyst / Inciting Incident (p. 12-15)
*The event that disrupts the ordinary world*

### Debate (p. 15-25)
*Protagonist resists the call to adventure*

### Break into Two (p. 25-30)
*Protagonist commits to the journey*

---

## Act Two-A: Fun & Games (Pages 31-60)

### B-Story Begins (p. 30-35)
*Introduce the subplot (often romantic or friendship)*

### Fun & Games (p. 30-55)
*The "promise of the premise" - the fun stuff from the trailer*

### Midpoint (p. 55-60)
*False victory or false defeat - stakes are raised*

---

## Act Two-B: Complications (Pages 61-90)

### Bad Guys Close In (p. 60-75)
*Internal doubts and external pressures mount*

### All Is Lost (p. 75-80)
*The lowest point - everything falls apart*

### Dark Night of the Soul (p. 80-85)
*Protagonist reflects, learns the lesson*

### Break into Three (p. 85-90)
*New idea / inspiration to try again*

---

## Act Three: Resolution (Pages 91-120)

### Finale (p. 90-115)
*Execute the plan, confront antagonist, apply lessons*

### Final Image (p. 115-120)
*Opposite of opening image - shows transformation*
